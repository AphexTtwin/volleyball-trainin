💡 Key Technique Tips

  • Use a fast but controlled last two steps during your approach.
  • Swing both arms aggressively upward during takeoff.
  • Jump straight up rather than drifting forward.
  • Keep your core tight throughout the jump.
  • Contact the ball at the highest point possible.
  • Reach high with the hitting shoulder and fully extend the elbow.
  • Snap the wrist over the ball for topspin.
  • Land softly on the balls of the feet before the heels touch.
  • Allow knees and hips to bend naturally during landing.

✅ Daily Training Checklist

  • Complete warm-up (10+ minutes)
  • Main jumping drills with proper technique
  • Spike mechanics practice (wall or net)
  • Core/strength work as prescribed
  • Cool-down stretching (5-10 minutes)
  • Land softly every time – no hard landings
  • Hydrate properly before/during/after

🛡️ Injury Prevention Notes

  • Never perform maximum jumping volume on consecutive days if experiencing soreness.
  • Prioritize landing mechanics before increasing jump intensity.
  • Keep knees aligned with toes during takeoff and landing.
  • Strengthen calves, glutes, hamstrings, and core regularly.
  • Include ankle mobility work before every jumping session.
  • If knee pain develops, reduce plyometric volume temporarily.
  • Sleep 7–9 hours per night for optimal recovery and adaptation.
  • Stay hydrated and maintain adequate protein intake.
🏐 Consistency beats intensity. Execute each rep with excellent technique and gradually increase difficulty over time.
